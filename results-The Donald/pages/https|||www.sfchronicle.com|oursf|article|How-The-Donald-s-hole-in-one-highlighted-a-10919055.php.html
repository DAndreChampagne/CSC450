How the Donald’s hole-in-one highlighted a Trump-ian Pebble Beach

Peter Hartlaub Feb. 9, 2017 Updated: Jan. 2, 2018 2:47 p.m.
More

Comments Print

For one day, at least, nobody could blame Donald Trump for boasting.

The biggest long shot of the businessman-turned-politician’s career — arguably bigger than being elected to the presidency — happened in 1993, on the 12th green of the Spyglass Hill Golf Course, at the AT&T Pebble Beach Pro-Am. That’s where Trump, during his first visit to the tournament that resumed this week, sank a hole-in-one.

It was a busy weekend for Trump, who was more than a decade away from “The Apprentice” and 24 years away from the White House, but he still managed to upstage every other celebrity and athlete at the golf tournament. (Final San Francisco Chronicle name-in-a-headline score: Donald Trump: 3; Bill Murray: 1.)

Adding to the drama: On the same day that he hit the miracle shot, his girlfriend, Marla Maples, reportedly surprised the tycoon, hoping for a golf course wedding ceremony that didn’t happen.

The hole-in-one came after Trump struck the ball 180 yards with a 5-iron, and it bounced once before dropping into the cup. His recap of the event was perfectly Trump-ian, focusing on crowd size, adulation and one solid name drop.

“It was just a great moment, in front of thousands and thousands of people,” Trump told The Chronicle’s golf writer Ron Kroichick in 2005. “The place went crazy, totally nuts. Payne (Stewart) was a friend of mine, and he said, ‘Slow down your swing, Donald.’ I didn’t know if I did slow up my swing, but the ball went in the hole.”

Donald Trump plays at the Spyglass course in the AT&T Pebble Beach Pro-Am in 1993. He hit a hole-in-one on the 12th hole.
Photo: Chris Stewart, The Chronicle

Trump justifiably recalled the shot to anyone who would listen, but apparently balked on the hole-in-one tradition of buying drinks for everyone back at the clubhouse.

“Even at a dollar a drink, with 25,000 people, that would be expensive,” Trump told Chronicle columnist C.W. Nevius. “I told them if they could prove they were there, I’d buy them a drink.”

The Chronicle sent a photographer out to follow Trump the next day, when photos show his luck was failing. The series of images by Chris Stewart show the future president grimacing on a putting green, or striking balls while deep in the woods.

After the golf part was done, Chronicle society columnist Pat Steger took over, with a fusillade of gossip.

“Donald Trump had a great time at … Pebble Beach,” Steger wrote, “but not such a great time was had by Marla Maples who joined him mid-tournament — which meant that the gorgeous blond model he brought with him, who does Frederick’s of Hollywood catalogs, had to go home to L.A.”

According to a “close friend,” Steger reported, Maples packed a wedding dress, expecting to marry Trump at 3 p.m. on a Saturday afternoon, but he called it off at the last minute.

Two months later, the news media reported that Maples was two months pregnant. The couple married the following December.

Trump would play Pebble Beach a few more times, ending his run in 2005, when he was building his own golf course in Rancho Palos Verdes (Los Angeles County). He didn’t predict another hole-in-one, but he did tell Kroichick he expected to hear chants of “you’re fired” from the gallery.

“Every time I miss a putt, they’ll be saying it,” Trump said. “I hear it all day long, everywhere I go. Everyone says it to me, whether I’m playing golf or walking down the street.”
