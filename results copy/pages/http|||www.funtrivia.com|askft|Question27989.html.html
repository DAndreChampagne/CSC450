The United States has had 52 Presidents, with 51 people holding the office. Let me explain this. We have the traditional 43 Presidents, plus 8 presidents that served under the Articles of Confederation before George Washington. In addition, we must also count David Rice Atchison, who was President of the United States for one day while Zachary Taylor was waiting to take the oath of office.

Re the 8 'presidents' prior to George Washington. Whilst it is possible to call these men 'American Presidents' or 'Presidents of America', they were definitely not Presidents of the United States. Prior to George Washington the 'United States' never existed. They may have been 'presidents' of the Disunited States!!
http://teacher.scholastic.com/researchtools/articlearchives/civics/presid/fun/prday.htm

A law had been enacted by Congress on March 1, 1792, that provided that "in case of the removal, death, resignation or disability of both the President and Vice President of the United States, the President of the Senate Pro Tempore shall act as President." The Vice President resides over the Senate. The Senate selects a President Pro Tempore, a temporary President, who presides during the absence of the Vice President.

David Rice Atchison was never actually sworn in as President, never signed any legislation, and never lived in the White House. This suggests he only 'acted' as President and I would suggest that, if he counts on this basis, then every vice president that has held the reins for a few hours/days must also be counted. This has happened on occasions that a President has gone into hospital for surgery requiring a general anaesthetic for instance.

John Hanson was president of the Continental Congress. He was NOT a US president, because under the Articles of Confederation, it was expressly stated that there was no executive branch and no president. This was done, erroneously IMO, because the leaders of the newly created country wanted nothing to do with the type of government that they left in England, and that they had just fought and won their independence from. A president seemed more like a king to them, and they wanted nothing that hinted of a monarchy. They were afraid of one person holding too much power. So the 1st president of the United States of America was indeed George Washington, the only president ever unanimously elected in US history. He was elected in 1789, shortly after the new US Constitution was drafted and voted into being in 1787. He presided over that Constitutional Convention. Interestingly enough, George Washington warned us of two things that we have not heeded very well: stay away from political parties, and stay out of foreign affairs.
~Wendy Hallmark, History teacher, Texas~

As of now President Obama is considered the 44th President of the United States. Paradoxically he is only the 43rd different person to be President. This is because the conventional count of Presidents has Grover Cleveland as both 22nd and 24th President. He was elected, then lost re-election but was elected again later. So he served two non-consecutive terms. My issue with this is that Historians apparently don't know how to count correctly.
