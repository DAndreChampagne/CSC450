Donald Trump is coming to southeastern Minnesota

  • Winona Daily News staff
  • Sep 26, 2018
  • 2

  • Email

President Donald Trump is coming to southeastern Minnesota.

The 45th president will hold a rally at 6:30 p.m. Thursday, Oct. 4, at the Mayo Civic Center in Rochester. Doors will open at 3:30 p.m. for general admission. You can register for tickets or to volunteer here.

Local

In visit to Winona, state GOP chair 'hopeful and optimistic' that White House will stump in southern Minnesota

  • JOHN CASPER JR. Winona Daily News

The visit comes a little more than three months after Trump visited Duluth in June, which was his first trip to Minnesota since becoming president. Vice President Mike Pence also visited twice this summer.

For Minnesota Republicans, this is a dream come true, state GOP chairwoman Jennifer Carnahan said in a released statement. We are jumping through the roof with excitement to welcome our great and fearless leader, President Donald J. Trump, back to Minnesota next week.

Local

In visit to Winona, Dan Feehan stresses what he's for, not what he's against

  • JOHN CASPER JR. Winona Daily News

The importance of Minnesota towards protecting the House and Senate this election cycle is evident, and we appreciate the Presidents support of our candidates and all Minnesotans.

There are four competitive Congressional races in Minnesota this election cycle, including one in the 1st District, which stretches the southern part of the state from border to border. Republican Jim Hagedorn won the nomination for the third straight cycle and will face former Obama administration official Dan Feehan to fill a seat held by Tim Walz, the DFL candidate for governor. Its considered one of the best opportunities for the GOP to flip a seat in the House as it tries to cling to its majority.

News

In visit to Winona, Jim Hagedorn believes third time's the charm in congressional race

  • JOHN CASPER JR. Winona Daily News

During a visit with the Winona Daily News after the primary elections in August, Carnahan was asked if the importance of the 1st Congressional District race would mean a visit from Trump or another White House official before November.

Were certainly hopeful and optimistic that well have national figures come back into Minnesota again before the general election in November, Carnahan said at the time. We try to promote and encourage and work on that with our partners out in D.C. Well have to see what happens.

There are other key state-wide races as well, including a race for an open governors seat, two U.S. Senate races and a high-profile battle for the states attorney generals office.
