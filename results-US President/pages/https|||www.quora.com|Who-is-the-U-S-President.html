Originally Answered: Who is the PM of the USA?

Prime Ministers are generally the Head of Government of their respective nations, while the position of Head of State is often granted to a separate individual. The Head of State, in a system with a Prime Minster, rarely has significant power, and is often a figurehead like Queen Elizabeth II.

On the other hand, in the United States, the Head of State is the President, who is also the Head of Government.

Therefore, there is no room for a Prime Minister in the American political system. The Head of Government in the United States is therefore Donald Trump.

However, it is worth noting that “Prime Minister” is often a term used for the leader of the lower house of Parliament; this is true in England and many ex-colonies of England, such as Canada. America, unlike England or Canada, has a Congress instead of a Parliament, and the election system by which one enters Congress is different.

The equivalent role to this in the United States, however, would probably be the Speaker of the House. Therefore, if we use this definition of “prime minister”, Paul Ryan is the closest thing America has to a Prime Minister.

Some people will argue that the Speaker of the House is actually more powerful than the President from a purely legal and constitutional standpoint. The reality, however, is that the President often has more influence and fame among the general population and in international relations.

Therefore, one could argue that Paul Ryan is our Theresa May, and that Trump is our Elizabeth II (but for a maximum of 7 years, of course), but that’s probably too simplistic a comparison.
