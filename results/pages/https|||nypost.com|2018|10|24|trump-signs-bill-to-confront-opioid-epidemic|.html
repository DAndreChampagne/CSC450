Trump signs bill to confront opioid epidemic

By Nikki Schwab

  • View author archive
  • Get author RSS feed

Contact The Author

October 24, 2018 | 10:19pm

Modal Trigger
President Donald Trump signs legislation combating the opioid epidemic, in the East Room of the White House. AP

President Trump signed legislation Wednesday to confront the opioid crisis.

Together, we are going to end the scourge of drug addiction in America, Trump said at the White House.

Nearly 48,000 people died last year from drug overdoses involving opioids. The legislation passed by large margins in Congress expands access for treatment for Medicaid recipients, cracks down on mailed shipments of illegal drugs, and adds funding so states can deal with the crisis.
