Poll: Florida Hispanics back Bill Nelson, Andrew Gillum

A new poll of Hispanic voters in Florida shows support for Democrats Bill Nelson and Andrew Gillum but also key splits in the Cuban and Puerto Rican communities.

In the Mason-Dixon Polling & Strategy poll of 625 registered Hispanic voters from Oct. 25 to 30, Nelson led Gov. Rick Scott in the U.S. Senate race 54 to 39 percent, with his biggest leads in Central Florida with 71 percent and with 61 percent of Puerto Ricans.

Scott was slightly ahead of Nelson with Hispanics in more conservative Southwest Florida, 47 to 46 percent, and led with Cubans 65 to 29 percent.
