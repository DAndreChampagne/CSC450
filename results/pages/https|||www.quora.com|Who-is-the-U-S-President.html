Originally Answered: Who is the US president?

The US president is the president of the United States of America.

The inaugural holder of the position was George Washington.

The incumbent holder of the position is Donald John Trump.

The record for most consecutive terms is Franklin Delano Roosevelt with 4.

The record for the least amount of time in office is William Henry Harrison with 31 days in office.

The only president to serve two non-consecutive terms was Grover Cleveland.

The only person to resign from his presidency was Richard Nixon.

8 presidents have died while in office. 4 were from natural causes and 4 were from assassination.

2 presidents (Johnson and Clinton) were impeached by the House of Representatives, but were acquitted by the Senate. Consequently, no president has been impeached.

The richest president is Donald John Trump.

The poorest president was Harry S. Truman.

The oldest president when elected to office was Donald John Trump (age 70).

The youngest president to assume office was Theodore Roosevelt (age 42).

The record for most presidents (former and incumbent) alive at the same time is six and is currently active with Jimmy Carter, George H. W. Bush, Bill Clinton, George W. Bush, Barack Obama and Donald Trump all living.

All 44 of them were the leader of this great country we call America.

Originally Answered: Who's the US president?
The US President is the Head of State, Head of Government, and Commander-in-Chief of the United States of America. He also has great influence over whichever political party he leads, such that he is a de facto leader of the party as well. He leads a nation of more than 300 million people, covering 3.8 million square miles (10 million square kilometers), and boasting both the largest economy and the most powerful military in the modern world. Since he is the most powerful man in America and America is an international leader in dozens of ways, he is often called the leader of the Free World. The strongest limit to this impressive array of powers is that his powers are limited by a Constitution that the American people greatly respect which gives both the Congress (like a parliament, except always led by a broad and divided coalition) and the Supreme Court the ability to overturn his decisions if he oversteps the constitutional limits of his power.

The current US President is Barack Hussein Obama, a man born in Hawaii to a white, well-educated mother and a black native of Kenya as father, educated at Columbia University (one of the United States' finest universities), and who emerged on the national scene from Chicago. He is the first American President to have a black parent, and thus is often referred to as the first black President. Despite my political differences with him, I have to admit he's done an overall excellent job as President despite great and often unfair challenges against him. He has done his party, his race, and his nation proud.

Originally Answered: Who is the PM of the USA?

Prime Ministers are generally the Head of Government of their respective nations, while the position of Head of State is often granted to a separate individual. The Head of State, in a system with a Prime Minster, rarely has significant power, and is often a figurehead like Queen Elizabeth II.

On the other hand, in the United States, the Head of State is the President, who is also the Head of Government.

Therefore, there is no room for a Prime Minister in the American political system. The Head of Government in the United States is therefore Donald Trump.

However, it is worth noting that Prime Minister is often a term used for the leader of the lower house of Parliament; this is true in England and many ex-colonies of England, such as Canada. America, unlike England or Canada, has a Congress instead of a Parliament, and the election system by which one enters Congress is different.

The equivalent role to this in the United States, however, would probably be the Speaker of the House. Therefore, if we use this definition of prime minister, Paul Ryan is the closest thing America has to a Prime Minister.

Some people will argue that the Speaker of the House is actually more powerful than the President from a purely legal and constitutional standpoint. The reality, however, is that the President often has more influence and fame among the general population and in international relations.

Therefore, one could argue that Paul Ryan is our Theresa May, and that Trump is our Elizabeth II (but for a maximum of 7 years, of course), but thats probably too simplistic a comparison.
