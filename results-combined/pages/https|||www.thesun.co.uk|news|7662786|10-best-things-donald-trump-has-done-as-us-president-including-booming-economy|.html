10 Best Things Donald Trump has done as US president including booming economy

The Republican's voting base love his America First jobs message while the country's economy has boomed during his two years in the White House

By Mark Hodge
6th November 2018, 3:36 pm
Updated: 6th November 2018, 3:53 pm

DONALD Trump is one of the most divisive US presidents in living memory – loved by his supporters and maligned by just about everyone else.

But let's cut through the negative coverage of his two years in office with a rundown of his most important successes while in the White House.

Maintains huge support among his base

Trump is a master at playing to his base of fervent supporters who love his "America First" policies and no-nonsense, combative style.

The brash New Yorker has kept many of his key election promises, some of which will be detailed below, including bringing thousands of manufacturing jobs back to the US from overseas.

A recent Gallup poll showed that Trump had a massive 87 per cent approval rating among his own voters.

That rating, taken at the 500 day mark of his presidency, is topped only by President George W Bush.

Bush enjoyed a 96 per cent approval among his base at the same stage – a figure which was boosted following the 9/11 terror attacks.

Strong US economy

Trump has spoken of his desire to turbo-charge the US economy which he claims is the healthiest it’s ever been.

It’s true that Americans are in the midst of a booming economy which includes low unemployment and a record-breaking stock market.

One of the key indicators is a strong Gross Domestic Product (GDP) - which under Trump has reached an annual growth rate of 4.2 per cent for the second quarter of 2018.

Although contrary to the president’s repeated claims, the US has been stronger economically at times in the 1950s and 1960s when GDP growth was higher and unemployment was lower than today’s figures, according to reports.

Low unemployment

Unemployment in the US, as of October, 2018, stands at 3.7 per cent which is the lowest since 1969.

But this is a downward trend which began during President Obama’s tenure in the White House.

Another statistic which Trump regularly highlights is the level of African American unemployment which at 5.9 per cent is the lowest since the 1970s.

Female unemployment is also at a 65 year low – a trend which again began during the Obama administration.

Soaring stock market

The country’s financial markets, particularly the Dow Jones Industrial Average, have experienced record highs under Trump.

Republicans point to the president’s tax cuts, his “America First” policies and his plan to invest in infrastructure to explain the growth in the stock market.

Donald Trump says he and North Korean leader Kim Jong-un 'fell in love' while negotiating over nuclear disarmament

Taming Kim

Trump’s meeting with Kim in Singapore in June was the first time a sitting US president has ever met a North Korean leader.

Following the summit, Kim vowed to destroy all his nukes with the American billionaire saying the process would start “very quickly”.

The US president said the meeting had gone “better than anyone could have expected.”

Despite Kim’s vow to disarm his nuke programme, US intelligence services claim that North Korea is still developing missiles.

Bringing home jobs

One of the reasons Trump sensationally beat Hillary Clinton in the normally Democratic states of Wisconsin and Pennsylvania was his "America First" jobs message.

Over recent decades America's manufacturing sector has suffered against increased competition from Asian countries.

Since he took office, Trump has followed through on his promise of bringing US jobs back from overseas market.

According to the Whitehouse.gov, 304,000 manufacturing have been created since the billionaire took office while employment in the sector stands at its highest level since December 2008.

Crackdown on MS-13 'animals'

Trump has launched a crackdown against MS-13 – the world’s largest and most violent street gang.

The gang, notorious for brutal killings, is believed to have an annual revenue of $31.2million, according to reports from El Faro newspaper.

In 2017, US authorities worked with Central American law enforcement to file criminal charges against more than 4,000 members of the notorious group.

In May 2018, Trump sparked controversy when an email to reporters referred to the members of MS-13 as "animals" a total of ten times.

The email read: "President Trump’s entire administration is working tirelessly to bring these violent animals to justice."

Supporters love tough immigration stance

While is Democratic opponents and many moderate voters would not consider Trump's "zero tolerance" approach to illegal immigration a success - his supporters would claim otherwise.

His election campaign was dominated by promises of a wall on the Mexican border and the highly controversial Muslim travel can.

And even though there is still no sign of the border wall, the firebrand leader has followed through with his hardline rhetoric.

In 2017, he banned eight countries, most of which were majority Muslim, from travelling to the US.

More recently he has deployed thousands of troops to the country's border with Mexico to deal with so-called migrant caravans from South America which are attempting to enter the US.

Return of Americans from overseas prison

In May, Trump's administration successfully negotiated the return of three Americans who were jailed in North Korea.

In the same month, one US national who was in prison in Venezuela was also allowed home.

According to the White House, a total of 17 Americans have been released from foreign jails during Trump's two-year tenure.

Appointing Neil Gorsuch to the Supreme Court

One of Trump’s first big moves as US President was appointing federal judge Neil Gorsuch to the Supreme Court.

The court is the most powerful in America and has the final say on many important issues such as abortion and gay marriage.

Trump supporters were overjoyed at the nomination of the conservative judge whose views include being staunchly against assisted suicide.
