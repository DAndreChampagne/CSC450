Today in History: November 8, 1988 — Bush defeats Dukakis in US presidential election

The 64-year-old vice-president won an easy victory over Dukakis, taking 40 of the 50 states

US President-elect George Bush.
Published: 18:26 November 7, 2018
Abdul Kareem, Head of Archive
 
  • SMALL
  • MEDIUM
  • LARGE

1988 - Americans voted for a successor to President Reagan. Republican George Bush was elected as the 41st president of the United States with a crushing victory over Democrat Michael Dukakis. The 64-year-old vice-president won an easy victory over Dukakis, taking 40 of the 50 states and chalking up a 54-46 per cent advantage in the popular vote. It marked the first time in 152 years that a sitting vice-president won the presidency.

Other major events on November 8

1793 - Louvre Museum in Paris opens to the public.

1889 - Montana becomes the 41st state of the United States.

1895 - Wilhelm Conrad Roentgen, German physicist, discovers X-rays.

1923 - Adolf Hitler stages unsuccessful coup in Munich, Germany.

1960 - John F. Kennedy beats Richard M. Nixon for the US presidency.

1995 -Sharjah Natural History Museum opens in Sharjah.

2003 - A car bomb detonates in a residential compound in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia, killing at least 17 people.

2013 - Nigeria wins the Fifa Under-17 World Cup in Abu Dhabi.

2016 - India scraps Rs500 and Rs1,000 notes to tackle corruption.

2017 - Spain’s Constitutional Court annuls Catalan declaration  of independence.
