        Speaking at his final campaign rally in Cape Girardeau, Missouri, the President told the crowd what will happen to him if the results of the elections are either good or bad for the Republicans in the state.

        He said: If we dont do so well tomorrow, they will put me on the ticket.

        If we do great tomorrow, they will say he has nothing to do with it.

        He made the comments in a rallying call to supporters suggesting if the Republicans had a bad night in Missouri they would want him to stand on their ticket at the next election in two years time.

        Mr Trump spoke before introducing his daughter Ivanka to address the rally.

        The rally lasted more than four hours with the President introducing a number of speakers including the Republican Attorney General Josh Hawley who is up against incumbent Democrat Senator Clair McCaskill.

        The Missouri rally was Mr Trumps third of the day, having already been to Ohio and Indiana, and finished after midnight.

        Americans are going to the polls today to elect new members of the House of Representatives and Senate.

        President Trump addresses a rally in Missouri (Image: GETTY)

        However the election is the first major test for President Trump and is widely being viewed as a referendum on peoples opinions of presidency.

        The Democratic Party and the Republican Party are fighting over 435 seats in the House of Representatives and 35 posts in the Senate.

        Pollsters have predicted Democrats will make gains but the extent of those gains remains a mystery until the polls close.

        If the gains are widespread it could spell disaster for the remaining two years of Trumps presidency as he would find it nearly impossible to pass legislation against a Democrat controlled Congress.

        President Trump speaks at a campaign rally earlier in the day in Indiana yesterday (Image: GETTY)

        However the more settled consensus among political analysts is that the race will be tight with the Republicans set to keep hold of the Senate but lose ground in the House of Representatives.

        Polls are now open and start closing about 11pm GMT with results expected to start coming in soon after.

        Ballot boxes in the key states of Georgia, Indiana, South Carolina and Virginia, which will give a clearer picture of how the night will unfold, close at midnight UK time.
