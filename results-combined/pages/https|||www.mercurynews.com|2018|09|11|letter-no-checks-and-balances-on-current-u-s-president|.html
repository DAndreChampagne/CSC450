Letter: No checks and balances on current U.S. president

The Republican Congress makes no move to reign in this president who has clearly run amok.

Share this:

  • Click to share on Facebook (Opens in new window)
  • Click to share on Twitter (Opens in new window)
  • Click to print (Opens in new window)
By Letters To The Editor |
PUBLISHED: September 11, 2018 at 7:36 am | UPDATED: September 11, 2018 at 7:52 am

No checks and balances
on current U.S. president

Re: “Thiessen: To the un-named op-ed writer: Serve honorably or not at all” (Opinion section, Sept. 7):

Referencing the anonymous op-ed writer, Marc Thiessen thinks that “Thwarting the president from within by extra-constitutional means is un-American” and “Subverting the president crosses a moral and constitutional line. You’re no longer defending democracy.”

Perhaps true in “normal” times, but we are far, far from normal when our system of checks and balances on this president are non-existent. The Republican Congress makes no move to reign in this president who has clearly run amok.

Huge, unprecedented numbers of people have resigned or been fired. That hasn’t worked. Neither would Thiessen’s suggestion that Cabinet members move to the 25th Amendment.

The fallacy in Thiessen’s arguments is that we do not have any checks and balances on this president. Who are insider administration people suppose to go to? No one is taking responsibility. So I, for one, am glad people on the inside are fighting for us.

Ron Geren
Livermore
