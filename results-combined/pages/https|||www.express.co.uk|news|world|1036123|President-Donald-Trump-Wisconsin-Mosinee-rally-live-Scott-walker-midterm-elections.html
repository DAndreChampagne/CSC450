    Donald Trump Wisconsin rally LIVE: US President speaks in Mosinee ahead of midterm polls

    PRESIDENT Donald Trump is in Mosinee, Wisconsin on Wednesday evening for his latest “Make America Great Again” rally as he campaigns for Republicans ahead of next month’s mid-term elections.

    By James Bickerton
    PUBLISHED: 05:02, Thu, Oct 25, 2018 | UPDATED: 05:05, Thu, Oct 25, 2018
    5
    5

    President Trump spoke at a rally in Wisconsin in support of Republican Governor Scott Walker (Image: GETTY )

    He is speaking in support of Republican Governor Scott Walker, who is seeking a third term in office, as well as underdog Republican Senate candidate Leah Vukmir.

    Governor Walker is engaged in a tough re-election battle against Democrat rival Tony Evers, currently the state superintendent for public schools.

    Governor Walker shot to fame after a very public battle with teaching unions and was considered a potential 2016 presidential candidate, however he dropped out of the race early in 2015.

    An NBC News poll conducted at the beginning of the month put Evers 10 points ahead of Governor Walker, with 53 percent to 43 percent. 

    Republican Leah Vukmir is challenging sitting Democrat Tammy Baldwin for his Senate seat, though the incumbent is very much the favourite to win.  

    The rally is taking place in a hanger at Central Wisconsin Airport.

    Follow below for live updates. All times in BST.

    2.16am update: Trump rally ends 

    President Trump’s rally ends with a vow to “Make America Great Again”. 

    Adds: “We will never surrender”. 

    The President is now heading towards his motorcade. 

    This marks the end of our coverage, thanks a lot for sticking with us!

    2.11am update: Audience member taken away ill 

    An audience member is taken away unwell. 

    President Trump repeatedly asks “do we have a doctor in the house”?

    The audience member’s condition is unclear. 

    Donald Trump is also supporting underdog Republican Senate candidate Leah Vukmir (Image: GETTY)

    2.06am update: Trump “We have a great relationship with Kim Jong-un"

    President Trump has just claimed: “We have a great relationship with Kim Jong-un.” 

    Adds South Korea needs to contribute more towards maintaining the American soldiers in their country. 

    Trump also claims he "turned his phone off" to relieve pressure from foreign leaders when the US moved its embassy in Israel in Jerusalem. 

    2.03am update: Trump launches assault on “globalists” 

    Trump “We had these globalists, they didn’t take care of us so well they wanted to take care of the globe. 

    “I want to care of the United States of America.”

    Adds that American allies need to pay more towards their own defence. 

    1.57am update: Trump claims American towns are getting "liberated" from MS13 "like in World War two"

    President Trump has claimed American cities and towns are being "liberated" from MS13 crime gang is a situation which reminds him of the second world war. 

    Also notes "Kanye West likes me" to wild applause from the crowd. 

    President Trump speaking in Wisconsin (Image: Right Side Broadcasting )

    1.50am update: Trump “Early voting looks extraordinarily good for the Republicans”

    Trump claims “early voting looks extraordinarily good for the Republicans” for the mid-terms. 

    Also accuses the Democrats of wanting to provide “medicare for illegal immigrants”. 

    Trump didn’t provide any proof for this assertion. 

    Trump: “As we speak the Democrat Party is encouraging caravan after caravan of illegal aliens. 

    “The crisis on the border is the direct result of Democrat laws and Democrat interventionist justice.”

    1.40am update: Governor Scott Walker and Leah Vukmir speak from podium

    Governor Scott Walker and Leah Vukmir took it in turns to spoke to the podium whilst Trump stands to one side. 

    Ms Vukmir says of Trump: “Everything he said he would do he has done”.

    1.32 update: Trump claims Democrat candidates wants “a socialist takeover of healthcare” 

    Trump says Democrat Tommy Baldwin wants “a socialist takeover of healthcare”. 

    Adds she voted in favour of “deadly sanctuary cities”. 

    Mr Baldwin is being charged by Republican Senate candidate Leah Vukmir. 

    Crowd chants "four more years". 

    Donald Trump said early voting looks 'extraordinarily good' for Republicans (Image: REUTERS)

    1.26am update: “These will be the elections of the caravans, Kavanaugh and common sense” 

    Trump claims “these will be the elections of the caravans, Kavanaugh and common sense”. 

    Repeats his endorsement of Republican Party candidates. 

    1.22am update: Trump claims Justice Brett Kavanaugh was “treated very unfairly” 

    President Trump welcomes Brett Kavanaugh joining the US Supreme Court. Claims he was “treated very unfairly” during the nomination process. 

    Trump: “And then I saw the way they made him suffer. But he got through it with style, he got through it with grace.” 

    1.18am update: Trump boasts about replacing NAFTA 

    President Trump says he has replaced the “horrible, horrible” NAFTA trade deal which he terms “a disaster”. 

    Claims his new deal with Canada and Mexico will be great for Wisconsin farmers. 

    Trump: "Now as you know the Canadian market which was not open is now open". 

    1.15am update: Trump promises “10 percent middle class tax cut” 

    President Trump says he will soon provide a “10 percent middle-class tax cut”. 

    However he provides no details or information about how this will be delivered. 

    President Trump said Supreme Court Justice Brett Kavanaugh was treated 'very unfairly' (Image: Golden State Times)

    1.09 update: Trump vows “very secure border” 

    President Trump vows the US will have a “very secure border”. Adds “the military are ready”. 

    Crowd chants “USA, USA”. 

    Claims “thousands and thousands of MS13, they’re out” referring to drug gang. 

    1.05 update: Trump endorses Republican candidates. 

    President Trump is urging the crowd to vote for Governor Scott Walker and Senate Candidate Leah Vukmir. 

    Trump said: “Republicans are for lower taxes, lower regulations and more economic freedom. 

    “The Democrats are for higher taxes, more regulation and more top down Government control.” 

    Trump goes on to attack illegal immigration saying "if you don't have a border, you don't have a country". 

    Crowds chanted 'USA, USA' when Trump promised to build a wall along the Mexico border (Image: REUTERS)

    0.59am update: President Trump condemns mail bombs

    President Trump has started speaking.

    Starts by addressing “suspicious devices” sent to “former high ranking officials”. 

    Says those responsible will be caught and brought to justice. 

    Trump states: “Any acts or threats of political violence are attacks on our nation itself.

     “Those active in the political arena must stop treating opponents as morally defective. 

    “We should not mob people in public spaces or destroy public property”. 

    Also calls on the media to stop the “constant false stories”.

    Crowd applauds his words. 

    0.50am update: President Trump leaves Airforce One! 

    President Trump has left Airforce One. 

    The rally should commence soon. 

    Trump supporters are have been repeatedly chanting the President's name.   

    Airforce One provided the backdrop to the Wisconsin rally (Image: Right Side Broadcasting Network )

    0.45am update: Press corps leaving Airforce One 

    The press corps which travelled with President Trump on Airforce One are currently leaving the aircraft. 

    It is parked behind President Trump’s podium, and will provide the backdrop to his speech. 

    0.37am update: Airforce One has landed! 

    Airforce One, carrying President Trump, has just landed in Mosinee, Wisconsin. 

    To reflect this the presidential seal has been added to the podium from which Mr Trump will speak. 

    Rally organisers are currently playing rock and pop music, with some Trump supporters waving banners to the music. 

    Trump fans are carrying signs saying “Jobs not Mobs” and “Keep America Great”. 

    0.25am update: President Trump to arrive shortly 

    Welcome! President Trump is due to arrive soon to thousands of supporters, many wearing his trademark red 'Make America Great Again' hats, in a hanger at Central Wisconsin Airport. 

    A reporter for pro-Trump Right Side Broadcasting Network estimates that "at least 5,000" people are in attendance. 

    Jonathan Allen, political reporter for NBC News, reported earlier: “More than two hours before Trump arrival, crowd at Mosinee, Wisconsin, rally chants ‘lock her up’.”
