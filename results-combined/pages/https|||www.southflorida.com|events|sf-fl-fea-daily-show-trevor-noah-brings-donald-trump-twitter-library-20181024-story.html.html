'The Donald J. Trump Presidential Twitter Library' has arrived in Miami Beach. Here is a look inside.

“The Daily Show with Trevor Noah,” will be in Miami Beach along with the Donald J. Trump Presidential Twitter Library. The library is a sarcastic take of a museum and presidential library, “showcasing our Commander-in-Chief’s preferred vessel for communicating with the public, his Twitter feed."

Walk into the “Donald J. Trump Presidential Twitter Library” and you’re greeted by a giant tweet on a white board.

“Many people have said I’m the world’s greatest writer of 140 character sentences,” reads Trump’s tweet from July 21, 2014.

Trump’s tweets from past and present are everywhere in this two-story exhibit, a satirical version of a presidential library and museum.

“We are living through a rare moment in history, possibly the last moment in history,” a video of “The Daily Show” host Trevor Noah greets visitors. “And thanks to Twitter, we have unprecedented access into an American president’s complicated inner life whether we want to or not.”

Miami Beach is the fifth stop of the Comedy Central library which is free and open to the public. The exhibit opened Friday morning and will stay open through 10 p.m. Friday, from 11 a.m. to 10 p.m. Saturday and from 10 a.m. to 10 p.m. Sunday at the Emanuel Luxury Venue, 1723 Washington Ave.

The library is across the street from The Fillmore Miami Beach where Noah will be broadcasting “The Daily” for a week of live shows beginning Oct. 29 with his team of correspondents.

“The exhibit as a whole is great because it really is a window into someone’s brain,” said “Daily Show” correspondent Roy Wood Jr. on Friday. “And if you look at everything in this Twitter library, you’ll see that a lot of random stuff is just ping-ponging around in [Trump’s] brain at all times.”

And if an alarm and flashing red light suddenly go off when you’re inside the library, don’t panic. The means that the President just tweeted in real time.

Here are some of the displays from the library’s 21-piece exhibit that we encountered with some of “Daily Show” correspondents:

Trump Nickname Generator

Ever wonder what the President might call you if got on his bad side? Then step up to the Trump Nickname Generator. Simply type in your first name and the computer whips up a Trump nickname for you such as Little Marco, Jowly Johnny, Baby Mike or Dummy Ronny. The latter is what “Daily Show” correspondent Ronny Chieng was given after he entered his name. On a second try, he got “InBred Ronny” which he didn’t like. “I’ll take the first one,” he said. A staffer will write your nickname on sticker for you to wear during your library visit.

The Commander-in-Tweet

This is the main display which is a replica of the Oval Office with blue carpeting, red rope and a coat hanger with red Make America Great Again caps and two blond wigs. Visitors can step right up on the stage where they will have 30 seconds to craft a tweet on a golden toilet during a crisis scenario.

“You can sit and Tweet like the President,” joked correspondent Desi Lydic who said that the nickname generator earlier called her Boneheaded Desi. “That was my nickname back in high school,” she said.

The World According To Trump

This is a giant map of 27 tweets that Trump has dispatched about other countries. Each one is marked with a numbered flag that corresponds to a Trump tweet. On Egypt, he wrote, “With allies like Egypt and Libya, who needs enemies?” On Iraq, Trump once Tweeted, “Iraq in political turmoil one day after we leave --- I told you so.”

Deleted But Not Forgotten

Covered in red roses, this display pays respect to eight of Trump’s deleted tweets. One from Aug. 2016 was about Miami Heat star Dwyane Wade. Trump wrote, “Dwayne Wade’s cousin was just shot and killed while walking her baby in Chicago. Just what I have been saying. African-Americans will VOTE TRUMP!”

Mar-a-Lago Lounge

This is a grand golden-hued door that theoretically opens to Trump’s winter White House in Palm Beach. The cost of admission: $200,000, the display reads. Next to the door are six of Trump’s tweets about the lavish private club. One from February 2017 states, “Meeting with Generals at Mar-a-Lago in Florida. Very interesting.”
