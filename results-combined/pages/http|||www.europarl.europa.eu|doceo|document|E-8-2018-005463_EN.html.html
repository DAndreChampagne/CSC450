Question for written answer E-005463/2018

to the Commission

Rule 130

Francisco Assis (S&D)


  Subject:              VP/HR - Intimidating statements by the US President against Central American migrants and their countries of origin

At the end of October, President Trump made intimidating and defamatory statements against a migrant caravan made up of thousands of Central Americans, mostly Hondurans, who are fleeing violence resulting from organised crime and an existence stripped of any real dignity. After these threats, Trump announced that he would cancel economic aid to the migrants’ home countries, specifically Honduras, Guatemala and El Salvador. Cutting or cancelling aid to these countries would only serve to worsen the socio-economic situation there and, as a consequence, increase the flow of people looking to leave. On the contrary, the only way to stall this dramatic exodus is to help with development and consolidate democracy and security. These desperate people, including many children, need help and protection rather than threats and deportations.

In the light of this, how does the EU intend to contribute to helping and protecting these people and making the North American authorities aware of their obligation to treat them humanely and in respect for international law on migration and asylum?
