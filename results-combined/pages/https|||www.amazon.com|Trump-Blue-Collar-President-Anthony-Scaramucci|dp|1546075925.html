About the Author

Anthony Scaramucci is an American financier, entrepreneur and political figure. He is the founder and co-managing partner of SkyBridge Capital, a global alternative investments firm.
As part of the first generation of his family to attend college, Scaramucci graduated from the Tufts University and Harvard Law School before embarking on a successful career in the financial services industry. In November 2016, Scaramucci was named to President-Elect Trump's Presdential Transition Team Executive Committee. In June 2017, he was named the Chief Strategy Officer of the Export-Import Bank. He served as the White House Communications Director for 11 days in July 2017.
In 2016, Scaramucci was ranked #85 in Worth Magazine's Power 100: The Most Powerful People in Global Finance. In 2011, he received Ernst & Young's "Entrepreneur of the Year-New York" Award in the Financial Services category. He is a member of the Committee on Foreign Relations (CFR).
Scaramucci was born and raised on Long Island, New York, where he still resides today.

I have been trying to figure out so much about our country lately including the hatred and the venom that is being spread. Normally, I don't read any type of political books but decided that I should in order to try and understand the current climate in the USA.

This book was an interesting read in that it covered Donald Trump's business deals before his Presidency. As early as the 1970's, Trump was becoming a champion of regular people in his real estate business.

The author's 'dismissal' from the White House after 11 days is covered at the end...Also, an interesting aspect of this book.

All in all, I thought the book was well written and the background on President Trump was presented with facts.

Highly recommended.

First-Gen-Immigrant, Father, Husband, Humanist, Moderate
5.0 out of 5 starsProvides insight into why Trump is president, to coastal people like me
October 23, 2018
Format: Hardcover
I'm a first-generation immigrant.
I live in Silicon Valley.
I work for a big tech.

Like many of my co-workers, I was surprised to see that Donald Trump became president.
Many of my co-workers staged protests against the Trump presidency.
I did not participate in those protests, I don't think that's productive.
Instead, I started reading books about him, about Democrats, about Republicans, about America, in an effort to understand the perspective of all sides of the argument, to understand what is going on, and maybe, to find some solutions to some problems that lead to where we are today.

This book is one of the best in terms of providing those answers.
Thanks to this book, I now understand why "Making America Great Again" is a slogan that can resonate.
Thanks to this book, I now understand why Trump voters don't care about his imperfections and incivility.
Thanks to this book, I now realize how ignorant I have been about the plight of the middle of America, a place my co-workers and I would sometimes arrogantly call "the fly over states".

If you were to view this period from the perspective of a future historian, the Trump presidency is a phenomenon caused by the transition "from exploitation to irrelevance" (A quote from Yuval Noah Harari's book "Sapiens: A Brief History of Humankind").
This is what happened in America:
The "aspiring working class", as this book calls them, was able to earn a life of relative dignity with their work ethic and labor-union protection. This is the "exploitation" phase, where workers are "exploited" but was paid enough to have a relatively good life.
Then comes the "irrelevance" phase: when all blue-collar jobs are lost (in the case of early 21st America, mostly due to out sourcing), the former working class became economically irrelevant. Life, for them, lost its original meaning that they have relied upon for a whole generation. When the "forgotten men and women" search for an answer, the easy answer would be to go back to a period which they understand, hence "Making America Great Again" is a slogan that easily resonate.
Trump is president:
* NOT because half of America is racist
* NOT because half of America is sexist
* NOT because half of America is xenophobic
* Because half of America lost the jobs they used to have
* Because half of America wants the prosperity they used to enjoy
* Because half of America don't see a better alternative other than Trump
And here's a piece of not-so-great-news for the rest of the world. What America is experiencing today, the transition from "exploitation to irrelevance", will eventually happen all across the world due to A.I (maybe around late 21st century? I don't know). America is suffering from this "exploitation to irrelevance" transition MUCH earlier than other countries because of out-sourcing. And a human political system simply is in-capable of rational and fast response to this kind of change.

Is there a solution? Yes, there are a lot of solutions. But all the solutions require one thing that we don't seem to have today: the willingness to understand the other side and not resorting to calling them "racist" just because you are in disagreement. Reading this book would be a good start, for coastal people like me.
