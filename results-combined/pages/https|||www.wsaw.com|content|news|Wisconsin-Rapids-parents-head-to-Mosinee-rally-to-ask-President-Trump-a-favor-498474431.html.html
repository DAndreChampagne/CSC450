Parents of Coast Guard Academy grad unsuccessful in getting signature
Parents Hope to Continue Service Celebration
By Emily Davies | 
Posted: Wed 8:53 PM, Oct 24, 2018  | 
Updated: Thu 12:43 PM, Oct 25, 2018

MOSINEE, Wis. (WSAW) -- Thursday, Oct. 25, 2018 --

The proud parents of two United States Coast Guard officers told NewsChannel 7 Thursday they were unable to get the photos of their children signed by the president.

President Donald Trump flew into the Central Wisconsin Airport in Mosinee Wednesday night for a rally to campaign for republican candidates in the state.

Thousands of people lined the airport to get a glimpse of the president, including Paul Gurtler and his wife.

The couple from Wisconsin Rapids tried keep with their presidential tradition that started with their daughter in 2015 when then-President Barack Obama signed her commission and graduation photos from the U.S. Coast Guard Academy. Their son, who graduated in 2017 from the academy met President Trump, but never got his photos signed.

Gurtler said Wednesday he and and his wife were able to speak with the White House Press Corp to ask if the photos and a poster could be passed along for the president to sign, but were told that was not possible at this event.

Gurtler said it was wishful thinking on their part, but still enjoyed having the opportunity to be so close to another sitting president again. He said they have tried mailing the photos with their request in the past, but they will try again.

________________________________________________________
Wednesday, Oct. 24, 2018 --

Thousands of people from all over the region gathered in Mosinee to get a glimpse of President Donald Trump. He is the first sitting president to visit central Wisconsin since 2004.

Among the crowd were two proud parents from Wisconsin Rapids hoping to continue a celebration of service that began with their kids three years ago.

"This is the first one when our daughter Victoria graduated in 2015 from the United States Coast Guard Academy," Paul Gurtler said showing a picture of his daughter with then-President Barack Obama. "In 2017, our son, Phillip graduated from the United States Coast Guard Academy and he received his diploma and his officer's commission from President Trump."

Though people were told not to bring signs into the rally Wednesday, Gurtler and his wife were on a mission, and hoped they would let their sign slide.

"President Obama signed Victoria's back in 2015 graduation photo and commission photo at the academy and we'd sure like to have this one of both our daughter and son, Phillip signed for graduation and commission in 2017," said Gurtler.

The Lincoln High School graduates are actively serving. Victoria is a commander of a Coast Guard Cutter in the San Francisco harbor and Phillip is stationed in Kodiak Island, Alaska.

No matter the political party in office, they serve, and their parents shamelessly show their pride.

"Commander in Chief was the one that granted both commission and the diploma for Victoria and Phillip," Gurtler explained. "Matters not the political affiliation, it's the Commander in Chief and it was a great day. So, we're just here to celebrate that one little piece again if we can."
