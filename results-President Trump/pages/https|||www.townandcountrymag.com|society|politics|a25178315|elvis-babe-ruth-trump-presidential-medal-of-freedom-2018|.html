Elvis and Babe Ruth Among Trump's Honorees for the 2018 Presidential Medal of Freedom

Trump awarded seven people the highest civilian honor today.

By Chloe Foussianes
Nov 16, 2018
Getty ImagesSAUL LOEB

At a White House ceremony this Friday, Donald Trump awarded seven Americans Presidential Medals of Freedom.

Three medals were given posthumously: to pioneering musician Elvis Presley, boundary-breaking baseball player Babe Ruth, and the late Supreme Court Justice Antonin Scalia. All other awardees—Senator Orrin Hatch, football players Alan Page and Roger Staubach, and Miriam Adelson—attended the ceremony in person.

Getty ImagesSAUL LOEB

"The Presidential Medal of Freedom has been given to individuals who have made outstanding contributions to American life and culture," said President Trump. "This year it is my true privilege to award this honor to seven extraordinary Americans... I want to congratulate all of this year's recipients, family members and loved ones."

Critics noted that Miriam Adelson was an odd pick. She is best known as the wife of GOP donor and Trump backer Sheldon Adelson. As a couple, they donated $30 million to Trump's 2016 campaign, and at least $113 million during the 2018 midterms. During the ceremony, however, Trump chose to highlight her accomplishments as a supporter of Israel and several Jewish organizations.

It's not unheard of for a President to give a Medal of Freedom to a donor. Barack Obama honored Oprah Winfrey, Ernie Banks, Ben Bradless, Daniel Kahneman, and Judge Patricia Wald with the Medal during his administration. All had donated to Democratic causes, and some directly to Obama's campaign.

The safest choices—the choices which all Presidents tend to make—are figures long beloved in the popular imagination, such as Presley and Ruth.

Here's the full list of recipients:

  • Miriam Adelson, wife of GOP donor and casino magnate Sheldon Adelson
  • Orrin Hatch, retiring Republican Senator from Utah
  • Alan Page, football player
  • Elvis Presley, musician
  • Babe Ruth, baseball player
  • Antonin Scalia, Supreme Court Justice
  • Roger Staubach, football player
